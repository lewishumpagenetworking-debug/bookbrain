Principle Review
The Curiosity-Gap Hook (Caples)
A curiosity hook works by opening an information gap — the reader knows enough to want more, but not enough to stop reading. Caples identified four core hook types: Self-interest, News, Curiosity, and Quick/Easy. The curiosity variant is the most dangerous because it gets clicks without delivering on desire — which is why most "native-style" copy fails: they open the gap but never close it with a real mechanism.
EXAMPLE (CAPLES ORIGINAL)
"They laughed when I sat down at the piano — but when I started to play!"
Notice: social tension → implied transformation → unresolved outcome → forces the next read.
Why it works: The brain cannot tolerate an open pattern. It must close the loop. The hook creates the loop. The lead must close it with the mechanism — not a vague promise.
Apply now: Every hook must have: (1) a specific tension or gap, (2) a specific implied payoff, (3) a reason the mechanism solves it.
